Introduction

The Gaming Academy was an initiative of twofour54 to stimulate talent growth in the creative industries, in particular video games. The course initally started in 2011 though I took over from 2012 and ran the course from both an administrative and content delivery perspective and managed stakeholder interest as Head of Academy. After my departure in 2015 the academy unfortunately wrapped up, though I have kept in contact with students and it has been exciting to watch their jounrey into the video games industry!

Creation of Curriculum

Initially, the curriculum for the course had been constructed from a handful of discrete units in order to just begin. Whilst these units gave some academic/theoretical grounding in game design, they weren't suited for either the timeframe of the course nor the desired practical, hands-on skills that employers generally seek in graduate developers - this coming from direct experience both hiring and applying! From this, the curriculum had a rewrite that would focus on a careful introduction of topics that considered any foundational and technical prerequisites along with a sober outlook as to just how far, in general, students could go with a particular subject over the course of 18 months. The units were designed to be complimentary and therefore were logically placed over the trimesters. The curriculum was also designed in a way that individual units, or logical groups, could be delivered independtly as short-courses. This modularity would help keep the Gaming Academy flexible and relevant whilst opening up other revenue streams using the same content.

Curse of Mermos

The students developed a game that was successfully released on Steam, the biggest digital games distribution platforms and has enjoyed more than 70'000 downloads. This was the students final project where everyone worked together for 4 months creating a fun and fast paced hack 'n slash title. I assissted in the production aspects of the project and practiced common industry methods for development transforming the classroom environment into a production studio simulation.
